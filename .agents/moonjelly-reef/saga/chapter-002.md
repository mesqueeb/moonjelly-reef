In the thin gold of morning, the diver knelt by the work ledge while the octopus held the lantern just above the tide pool, and the light fell back in a narrow shape that did not shimmer like water at all but stood still, clean-edged and patient. Neither of them spoke; the octopus only adjusted the copper coil by a hair, and the bright outline deepened against the stone as if the reef had finally decided where a threshold ought to be.
